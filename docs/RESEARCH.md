# Phase 1 — Market Research: Extracted Principles

Findings distilled from studying the leading U.S. platforms (Zillow, Redfin,
Realtor.com, Homes.com, Movoto, Compass) and luxury brokerage sites. Layouts
were not copied — only principles were extracted.

## Home discovery

- **Search is the product.** Every major platform leads with a single
  prominent search input; secondary filters come after intent is captured.
- **Cards are decisions.** Buyers shortlist from cards alone: photo, price,
  beds/baths/sqft, and address must be scannable in under a second. Price is
  the visual anchor.
- **Save early, save everywhere.** Favoriting from the card (not just the
  detail page) measurably increases return visits.

## Luxury patterns

- Restraint reads as expensive: generous whitespace, one serif display voice,
  muted palettes with a single warm accent.
- Photography-first layouts; chrome recedes. Where photos are absent, the
  placeholder must still look composed — never broken.
- Motion is slow, eased, and rare. Nothing bounces.

## Detail pages

- The gallery is the conversion surface: full-bleed, swipeable, keyboard
  accessible, with thumbnails and a full-screen mode.
- Progressive disclosure: overview → features → neighborhood → financials →
  agent. Long pages work when each section has a clear header rhythm.
- A mortgage estimate near the price reduces bounce; buyers translate list
  price into monthly cost immediately.

## Search & filtering

- Desktop: persistent sidebar filters beside results. Mobile: a bottom
  drawer with an "Apply" commit action — never inline accordions.
- Filter state must live in the URL so results are shareable and the back
  button works.
- Sort defaults matter: "Newest" for repeat visitors, "Recommended" for new.

## Journeys

- **Buyers** need saved homes, saved searches, and tour scheduling.
- **Sellers** need valuation confidence and listing performance visibility.
- **Agents** need pipeline and lead views that respect their time.

## Trust

- Real numbers (days on market, price history, area stats) build more trust
  than adjectives.
- Testimonials work when specific and attributed; stars alone do not.
- Clear data provenance and fair-housing compliance messaging are table
  stakes in the U.S. market.

## Performance & accessibility

- Core Web Vitals correlate directly with lead volume; LCP is almost always
  the hero or first card image — reserve dimensions, lazy-load the rest.
- Keyboard-complete galleries and filter forms; visible focus; honored
  `prefers-reduced-motion`; 4.5:1 minimum contrast.
